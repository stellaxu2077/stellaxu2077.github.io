**Why I Chose Bioinformatics: A Path Born from Exploration and Inspiration**

I grew up in a small coastal fishing town in eastern China, raised by my grandmother, who has battled chronic autoimmune diseases like rheumatoid arthritis for decades. Witnessing her struggles inspired me to pursue a career in biology, with the hope of contributing to treatments that could help people like her.

From 2018 to 2022, I completed my bachelor’s degree in Biological Sciences in Shanghai. However, during my studies, I found myself frustrated with wet lab experiments—I wasn’t as skilled at them as I had hoped. My turning point came when I joined my university’s team for the International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) competition. Our project focused on engineering bacteria to enhance calcium absorption as a potential treatment for osteoporosis in elderly Asian populations. That was my first introduction to mathematical modeling for simulating biological phenomena, and I was fascinated by how computational tools could be used to represent complex biological systems and solve real-world problems.

Motivated by this discovery, I began self-teaching programming languages including Python, R, and shell scripting. I applied these skills to small projects, such as analyzing single-cell transcriptomic data to identify differentially expressed genes and potential biomarkers. 

For my bachelor’s thesis, I explored various algorithms for predicting tumor antigen binding with HLA molecules, a key aspect of vaccine design and immunotherapy. Through this, I delved deeper into the applications of machine learning in biomedical research, which solidified my passion for bioinformatics.



**How Denmark Became My New Beginning: Determination in Adversity**

Initially, I was admitted to a highly regarded Master’s of Research program in Bioinformatics at another university in Shanghai. However, in 2022, during the citywide lockdown, I experienced severe depression. At my lowest point, I felt an urgent need for change—a desire to create a better environment for myself. Determined to take control of my future, I began researching universities abroad and submitting applications.

In 2023, I received several admission offers and chose the University of Copenhagen. I had long heard about the reputation of multinational pharmaceutical companies like Novo Nordisk, and Denmark’s thriving biopharmaceutical industry deeply resonated with my career aspirations. I believe that only in a place with strong industrial prospects can fundamental research be effectively translated into real-world applications, ultimately benefiting more people. Moreover, I hoped to transform my mental struggles into strength by contributing meaningful value to society. Additionally, the program’s flexibility allows me to maximize my credits by conducting research aligned with my interests, paving the way for my future career.



**A Health Scare That Changed My Perspective**

Just a month after receiving my admission offers, I was diagnosed with a small tumor. At the time, I was overwhelmed with anxiety—being in my twenties, I couldn’t help but fear the worst, worried that a serious diagnosis might render all my past efforts meaningless. Fortunately, after undergoing surgery and further tests, the tumor was confirmed to be benign. This experience, however, left a deep impact on me. As a patient, I came to truly understand the profound challenges faced by those whose lives are severely disrupted by illness. It became clear to me how much external support they need to navigate such difficult times.

In August 2023, I took a leap of faith and flew to Copenhagen on a 14-hour flight, traveling farther from home than ever before. It was a moment of both fear and determination—I knew I was embarking on an entirely new path, one that would be fundamentally different from the helplessness I had experienced before.
